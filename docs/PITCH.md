## Shark Tank Format (2 minutes)

> "Every weekend, 10 million Indians go on group trips — treks, road trips, beach getaways. And every single one of those trips is being organized on WhatsApp.
>
> The organizer — a 24-year-old guy who loves travel — is running a business on a chat app. He's collecting payments on UPI with zero contract, managing bookings in a Google Sheet, and spending 4 hours a day answering the same questions on Instagram DMs.
>
> The traveler? They're sending ₹15,000 to a stranger's UPI ID based on a screenshot. No receipt. No cancellation policy. No protection. If the organizer cancels the trip, they're chasing a refund on WhatsApp.
>
> **This is a ₹45,000 crore market — and it runs on trust, luck, and screenshots.**
>
> We built [AppName] — India's first marketplace for group travel. Organizers list their trips, travelers discover and book with seat selection and Razorpay payments. Money is held via SafePay until the trip is done. Cancellations are handled automatically. No WhatsApp required.
>
> We're not building a travel agency. We're building the infrastructure layer for the group travel economy that already exists — and has no platform to run on.
>
> We are raising ₹[X] crore for [X]% to sign the first 500 organizers across Maharashtra, expand to 5 cities, and put ₹1 crore into performance marketing to own this category before anyone else does.
>
> The window is open right now. Nobody has done this for India. We have."

## Full Pitch — VC / Angel / Demo Day (10–12 minutes)

---

### Slide 1 — Open with a story, not a stat

> "I want you to meet Rahul. 26 years old. Pune. He quit his IT job 18 months ago to run group trips full time.
>
> He organizes 4 trips a month — Ladakh, Spiti, Coorg, Goa. 20 to 30 travelers per trip. He's genuinely good at this. His Instagram has 40,000 followers who trust him.
>
> Here's how Rahul runs his business today:
>
> Someone DMs him on Instagram. He replies with the itinerary PDF he made in Canva. They say yes. He shares his UPI QR code. They send ₹5,000 as advance. He notes their name in a WhatsApp group. Then another person messages. And another. By the time the trip leaves, he's tracked 25 travelers across 3 WhatsApp groups, 2 spreadsheets, and 47 DM threads.
>
> On day one of the trip, he's standing at the bus counting heads because he has no other system.
>
> Rahul is not a bad organizer. Rahul is an organizer without infrastructure.
>
> And there are 50,000 Rahuls in India."

---

### Slide 2 — The Problem (3 layers)

**Layer 1 — Discovery is broken for travelers**
> "If you want to go on a group trip to Spiti, your only options are googling blogs, scrolling Instagram, or asking in a Facebook group. There is no Amazon for group travel. No single place where you can compare 5 different Spiti trips by price, itinerary, ratings, and organizer track record — and book the one you trust."

**Layer 2 — Operations are broken for organizers**
> "Every organizer in India is running a ₹50 lakh/year business on WhatsApp + UPI + Google Sheets. There is no booking system, no seat management, no cancellation policy enforcement, no payment protection. Organizers spend 30–40% of their time on admin work that should be automated."

**Layer 3 — Trust is broken on both sides**
> "Travelers are sending money to strangers with no receipt and no recourse. Organizers are chasing payments and dealing with last-minute cancellations with no policy to fall back on. The entire market runs on informal trust — which means it cannot scale."

---

### Slide 3 — The Market

> "India has 1.4 billion people. Weekend travel is exploding — post-COVID, domestic tourism grew 20% year-on-year. The group travel segment — treks, road trips, backpacking, cultural tours — is estimated at **₹45,000 crore annually** and growing at 18% per year.
>
> The average group trip is ₹12,000–₹25,000 per person. 20–30 travelers. That's ₹2.5–₹7.5 lakh per trip. An organizer running 4 trips a month is doing ₹1–3 crore in GMV annually — through WhatsApp.
>
> We are not creating a new market. We are building a platform for a market that already exists, already has demand, already has supply — and has zero infrastructure."

| Segment | Size |
|---------|------|
| Indian domestic tourism (annual) | ₹2.3 lakh crore |
| Group/experiential travel segment | ₹45,000 crore |
| YoY growth rate | 18% |
| Addressable organizers (India) | 50,000+ |
| Avg organizer GMV/month | ₹15–75 lakh |

---

### Slide 4 — The Solution

> "[AppName] is a two-sided marketplace for group travel."

**For travelers:** discover, compare (up to 3 trips side-by-side), book, select seats, pay via Razorpay with SafePay protection, cancel with automatic refund policy.

**For organizers:** list trips, receive bookings automatically, manage seat layouts, collect payments, release traveler details — one dashboard replaces 5 WhatsApp groups and 2 spreadsheets.

| Differentiator | Why it matters |
|----------------|----------------|
| SafePay payments | Traveler money protected; organizer trust is earned |
| Anti-leakage chat filter | Blocks phone/UPI/email in chat — keeps transactions on-platform |
| Seat layout builder | Organizers map their bus; travelers pick seats at booking |
| Trip comparison | Travelers compare up to 3 trips side-by-side |
| Auto trip lifecycle | Trips auto-complete; SafePay releases automatically |
| Request-based booking | Organizers approve travelers before payment (curated groups) |

> "Three things that are hard to copy: the SafePay trust layer, the anti-leakage filter, and network effects — the more organizers we have, the more travelers come, the more organizers want to list."

---

### Slide 5 — Business Model

**1. Marketplace commission — 5–8% per booking**
> "On a ₹15,000 trip with 25 travelers, that's ₹18,750–₹30,000 per trip. One active organizer running 4 trips/month = ₹75,000–₹1.2 lakh monthly revenue to us."

**2. Organizer SaaS subscription (Phase 2)**
> "Advanced analytics, early bird pricing, priority listing. ₹999–₹2,999/month."

**3. Travel insurance & add-ons (Phase 3)**
> "Trip cancellation insurance co-branded with an insurer. High-margin, zero inventory."

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Organizer CAC | ₹2,000–₹5,000 |
| CAC payback period | < 2 weeks |
| Organizer LTV (2 years) | ₹15–18 lakh |
| Take rate | 5–8% |
| Blended margin | ~70% |

---

### Slide 6 — Why Now

> "**One** — UPI normalized paying strangers online. The trust barrier for digital payments is gone. The behavior already exists, it just needs a platform with protection around it."

> "**Two** — Instagram created a generation of micro-organizers. They have audiences but no business tools. They're looking for infrastructure."

> "**Three** — Post-COVID domestic travel exploded and has not slowed down. The weekend trip is now a lifestyle category, not an occasional luxury. The demand is structural, not cyclical."

---

### Slide 7 — Traction

- Full platform built and deployed — booking flow, seat selection, SafePay payments, real-time chat, organizer dashboard, admin panel
- [X] organizers onboarded across Pune
- [X] trips listed, [X] bookings completed
- ₹[X] GMV processed
- Zero payment disputes to date — SafePay model works
- [Insert organic growth metric]

> "We're pre-scale but post-product. The infrastructure is built and working. What we need now is distribution."

---

### Slide 8 — The Ask

> "We are raising **₹[X] crore** at a **₹[X] crore pre-money valuation**."

| Use | Amount | Outcome |
|-----|--------|---------|
| Organizer acquisition (5 cities) | ₹X | 500 active organizers in 12 months |
| Performance marketing | ₹X | 50,000 registered travelers |
| Team (growth, ops, tech) | ₹X | Scale without breaking |
| Runway | ₹X | 18 months to Series A metrics |

> "At 500 active organizers each running 3 trips/month at ₹15,000/trip with 20 travelers — that's **₹45 crore GMV/month**. At 6% take rate, that's **₹2.7 crore MRR**."

---

### Slide 9 — The Close

> "Group travel in India is a massive, growing, informal market with no platform. The organizers want infrastructure. The travelers want trust. Both sides are ready for a solution. Nobody has built it for India at scale.
>
> We have the product, the thesis, and early proof. What we need is a partner who can see that the opportunity window doesn't stay open forever — the first mover in this category will be very hard to displace.
>
> We're not asking you to believe in a concept. We're asking you to help us scale something that already works."

## 3 Questions Sharks Will Definitely Ask

**"What stops MakeMyTrip from doing this?"**
> "MakeMyTrip sells inventory — flights, hotels, packages. Building a marketplace for independent organizers is a different business model, different ops, different seller relationship. It would cannibalize their existing margins. Plus — they've had 20 years and haven't done it."

**"What's your moat? Can't someone copy this in 6 months?"**
> "The product can be copied. The organizer relationships and traveler trust cannot. Every organizer we sign is a relationship. Every completed booking with zero disputes is a trust deposit. Network effects mean the 100th organizer is more valuable than the first — because travelers follow the supply."

**"Why are you the right team?"**
> "We understand both sides of this market because [personal connection / previous venture / domain experience]. And we've already built and shipped the full platform — we're not here with a deck, we're here with a working product."
